About Me

I’m Mohammed Elmir, an Electrical and Electronics Engineering graduate from Ashesi University, Ghana (Cum Laude, GPA 3.56/4.0*).
My research interests lie in Embedded Machine Learning, Internet of Things (IoT), and Hardware Acceleration, with a focus on FPGA-based architectures for low-power, high-efficiency computation at the edge.

Teaching & Research Background

As a Research and Teaching Assistant at Ashesi University, I supported both instruction and research within the Electrical and Electronics Engineering Department, focusing on Embedded Systems, IoT, and Applied Programming.

Teaching Experience

  • Assisted in delivering undergraduate courses in Embedded Systems, IoT, Applied Programming, and Introduction to Engineering.
  • Conducted lab sessions and hands-on demonstrations on microcontrollers, circuit design, and hardware–software integration.
  • Mentored students on project implementation, debugging, and embedded hardware design.
  • Provided academic support through tutorials, grading, and feedback on student projects.

Research Experience

  • Contributed to projects on FPGA acceleration, low-power TinyML, and IoT system optimization under the Embedded Systems and Smart Technology Research Group, supervised by Prof. Nathan Amanquah (Dean, EEE).
  • Led experimental verification and data analysis for publication-ready research.
  • Currently exploring hardware–software co-design for energy-efficient ML at the edge.
  • Co-led Ashesi Ghana’s iGEM Team (2023), integrating synthetic biology, image processing, and embedded systems, earning West Africa’s first iGEM Gold Medal.
