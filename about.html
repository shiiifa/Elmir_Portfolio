About Me

I’m Mohammed Elmir, an Electrical and Electronics Engineering graduate from Ashesi University, Ghana (Cum Laude, GPA 3.56/4.0*).
My research interests lie in Embedded Machine Learning, Internet of Things (IoT), and Hardware Acceleration, with a focus on FPGA-based architectures for low-power, high-efficiency computation at the edge.

Teaching & Research Background

As a Research and Teaching Assistant at Ashesi University, I supported both instruction and research within the Electrical and Electronics Engineering Department, focusing on Embedded Systems, IoT, and Applied Programming.

Research Vision

I aim to develop embedded and reconfigurable systems that integrate machine learning and image processing for adaptive, energy-efficient, and intelligent computation.
My goal is to enable hardware-level intelligence—systems that can sense, analyze, and respond in real time within constrained environments.
I’m particularly interested in how these technologies can advance digital VLSI, electronic system design and bioengineering, from hardware-accelerated image analysis and intelligent sensing to data-driven modeling of biological and environmental systems.
